Nicole E. Crawford

Psychotherapist

Fair Play Facilitator

I am a trauma-informed psychotherapist who utilizes a somatic and psychodynamic approach. I explore past and present identity formation, intimacy patterns, and personal metaphors to help my clients gain better understanding of the rest of their stories. With nonjudgmental perspective exploration I engage the unconscious through creative invitation and the body. I often partner with people who are experiencing the confusion and shame that comes from living with persistent emotional hunger pangs. At the core of my therapeutic work, I facilitate the healing process between my clients and their deep desire for a well-fed life.

As a certified Fair Play Method Facilitator I support individuals in a myriad of relationship situations in the naming and navigation of physical, mental, and emotional symptoms that come from persistent relational imbalances. I am passionate about shedding light on invisible labor that, when left unacknowledged, can lead to disconnection and burnout. I believe in the integral, connective role of embodied androgyny in healthy relationships. By offering Fair Play as a lens, I aim to empower my clients by raising awareness around how gender and race impacts privilege, diagnosis, social credibility, and cultural progress.

I have experience working with depression, anxiety, relationship issues, grief & loss, parenting/co-parenting stressors, and life transitions. I hold a Master of Arts in Depth Psychology with an emphasis in Somatic Studies from Pacifica Graduate Institute (2014) and a Master of Science in Clinical Mental Health Counseling from The University of Nebraska at Omaha (2018.)
